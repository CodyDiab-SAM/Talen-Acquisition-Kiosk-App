F1 - Walkthrough
F1 - Walkthrough

In early 2022, SAM was approached by Kimley-Horn to develop an innovative surveying and topographic mapping solution for a confidential project in Las Vegas—later revealed to be related to Formula 1. The assignment required achieving 95% accuracy at 1cm or less across more than 4 miles of roadway and 430 acres of land, all in one of the most densely trafficked corridors in the country: the Las Vegas Strip. With pedestrian congestion, vehicle flow, and proximity to Harry Reid International Airport creating major logistical hurdles, traditional GNSS methods alone wouldn’t suffice. The SAM team faced a tight timeline and high expectations under strict NDA conditions.

To overcome these challenges, SAM engineered a hybrid survey strategy that integrated aerial LiDAR, mobile mapping, terrestrial scanning, and high-resolution imagery into one cohesive 3D dataset. All data were referenced to a high-accuracy geodetic control network, ensuring exceptional precision despite the Strip’s complex urban environment. This blended geospatial model allowed SAM to meet rigorous standards without requiring road closures or disrupting local businesses—delivering world-class results in the heart of one of the busiest entertainment districts on the planet.

Francis Scott Key Bridge
Francis Scott Key Bridge

When the Francis Scott Key Bridge collapsed on March 26, 2024, after being struck by a powerless container ship, it triggered a major crisis for Baltimore’s infrastructure. The urgent need for accurate and timely geospatial data became critical, prompting rapid mobilization of agencies like the Maryland State Highway Administration and the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers. SAM, under the leadership of Justin Lahman, responded immediately by coordinating an aerial data capture mission using advanced LiDAR and imaging systems, delivering detailed imagery within 12 hours of the incident. This data provided valuable insights to support traditional mapping methods and inform rapid response decisions.

Beyond data collection, SAM played a pivotal role in coordinating with agencies including MDTA, MPA, and the Army Corps of Engineers. Their imagery not only supplemented sonar-based assessments but was also used publicly to communicate the extent of the damage. SAM's involvement showcased how modern geospatial technologies can accelerate recovery efforts through real-time collaboration and high-quality data. Their support and readiness helped streamline emergency response operations and improved the effectiveness of interagency communication.

As the recovery shifted toward reconstruction, SAM continued to contribute, working with stakeholders like Kiewit, the firm selected to lead the rebuild. The collapse had far-reaching impacts, disrupting commuter routes, port operations, and the regional economy. SAM’s swift and strategic response underscored its vital role in infrastructure resilience, proving that its value lies not just in data delivery, but in trusted partnerships and long-term recovery support.
